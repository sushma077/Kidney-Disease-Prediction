Some tips to prevent Kidney Disease

Eat Kidney-Friendly Diet

Eat fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.Reduce salt intake.Limit processed foods and high-sodium snacks.Balance your intake of protein-rich foods like red meat.

Stay Hydrated

Drink enough water to help your kidneys flush out toxins. Aim for 8-10 glasses a day unless advised otherwise by a healthcare provider.Avoid excessive consumption of sugary and carbonated drinks.

Maintain Blood Pressure

High blood pressure is a leading cause of kidney disease. Keep your blood pressure below 130/80 mm Hg through diet, exercise, and medication if needed

Control Blood Sugar Level

Diabetes is a major risk factor for kidney disease. Manage blood sugar through regular monitoring, a healthy diet, and medication as prescribed.

Limit Over-the-Counter Pain Medications

Long-term use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), like ibuprofen or aspirin, can harm the kidneys. Use them only as needed and under medical supervision.
